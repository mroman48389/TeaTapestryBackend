
# Tea Naming Conventions 

> The name column is the user-facing identity of the tea. Names must be canonical, non-ambiguous, and align with what the
> user might reasonably expect.

## 1. Cultivar-based Teas

> Use the cultivar name without appending the tea type. In Chinese and Taiwanese tea culture, the cultivar implies the tea type. Adding it to the end of the tea name isredundant and unnatural.

> Examples
>
>    1. Si Ji Chun
>    2. Ben Shan
>    3. Tie Guan Yin
>    4. Jin Xuan 

## 2. Style-based and Regional Teas

> Some teas are typically named after a style, shape, or region. Keep the tea type in the name for these teas to avoid ambiguity.

> Examples
>
>    1. Yunnan Snow Dragon White Tea
>    2. Taoyuan Da Ye Green Tea
>    3. Laoshan Green Oolong
>    4. Yunnan Black Gold
>    5. Vietnamese Silver Tips

## 3. Vendor-invented Names

> Some (typical more unusual and recent) teas lack canonical names and are sold under poetic vendor names. If the teas have some standing and are reproducible over the years, we allow them to become part of the database and add the tea type to avoid ambiguity.

> Examples
>
>   1. Jade Dragon Green Tea
>   2. Jade Earrings White Tea

## 4. Duplicate Tea Names Across Multiple Types

> Some teas are processed as multiple types of teas. They can occupy separate rows in the database so long as their types are different.

> Examples
> 
>   1. Gui Fei (oolong or black types)
>   2. Mao Feng (green or black types)

## 5. Dang Cong Teas

> Dan Cong teas are named after cultivar-derived aroma types. For these, use the the full aroma name + Dan Cong.

> Examples
>
>    1. Mi Lan Xiang Dan Cong
>    2. Yuang Zhi Xiang Dan Cong
>    3. Xing Ren Xian Dan Cong
>    4. Ba Xian Dan Cong

## 6. Japanese Teas

> Japanese teas are standardized as style + region/cultivar.

> Examples
>
>    1. Gyokuro Shizuoka 
>    2. Sencha Ashikubo
>    3. Ureshino Tamaryokucha
>    4. Fukamushi-cha

## 7. Vietnamese Teas

> Conventions are less established. In English-language markets, these teas tend to have English style names. Use that unless further clarity is in order.

> Examples
> 
>    1. Vietnamese Silver Tips
>    2. Vietnamese Wild Black
>    3. Tan Huong
>    4. Vert du Vietnam
